About The STEM Club

A focused nonprofit expanding access to applied STEM education.

We design learning environments where curiosity becomes capability: learners investigate, prototype, test, and improve with guidance from mentors and peers.

Our workshops emphasize durable technical habits: analytical thinking, evidence-based iteration, clear documentation, and responsible use of modern tools.

We support students, families, educators, and volunteers with structured programs that connect STEM concepts to real-world engineering challenges.

Technical workshops

Mechanical

Students explore how real-world products and machines are designed,built, and manufactured through hands-on engineering projects. They develop technical design skills using drafting, CAD Modeling, fabrication, and 3D printing technologies.

Electrical

Students learn how electricity, circuits, and electronic systems work through practical experimentation and circuit design. They build problem-solving skills by creating and testing functional electronic prototypes and systems.

Computer

Students develop computational thinking and coding skills by learning programming, logic, and software development concepts. They apply these skills to create interactive programs and control hardware through embedded systems projects.
